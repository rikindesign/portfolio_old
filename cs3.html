Transitioning our team from working at home to in-person work post COVID.
Role
UX / UI Designer
Timeline
Summer 2020
Tools
Figma, Sketch, InVision, Maze
Context
The WSIB Innovation Lab operates out of Communitech, a digital hub. During Summer 2020, I worked on Safe@Lab, a product focused on transitioning our team from work-from-home to in-person work post COVID as Ontario goes through each stage of the pandemic.

This project is going to be used as a test to see how contract tracing can working in a dynamic work environment with the WSIB Innovation Lab before we considering implementing it into internal WSIB to over 4000 employees.

I worked with another designer and closely with the development team to design a seamless experience, from onboarding, to bookings, and viewing attendance. The app shows which staff members are coming into the office on which day, and uses contact tracing technology.
Problems
The WSIB has assigned the Lab to create a product which uses contact tracing to help keep workers safe as they transition back to in-person work.

The WSIB Innovation Lab wants its members stay safe and comply with Communitech standards when we return to in-person work. A part of this is limiting in-person interactions while enhancing the experience of coming into work during the latter stages of the pandemic.

COVID-19 has caused many business to shift the way in which employees work. Many companies are even considering shifting to permanent work-from-home (WFH).

As Ontario enters Stage 3, Communitech has recently opened its doors and has allowed a minimal number of employees come in-person. However, in a future filled with uncertainty of when in-person work will resume, the Lab needs to ensure this transition is safe.
Our Team
The Lab is split up into core teams, which include Design, Communications and Development. Since each team has an associated lead with the lab staff reporting to that team lead, the core needs of these two types of users would be different.
Assessing Comfort
I wanted to talk to my fellow Lab members to better understand the gains, pains, and things necessary to enable in-person work.

Questions I had
  1. How comfortable are they returning?
  2. How long would they want to be there for?
  3. Users want a more personal interaction
  4. What are some problems you've run into when working from home?
  5. What would make you come in-person vs working from home?

Takeaways
  1. Most were open to the idea of safely returning to the Lab
  2. Wanted to stay for many hours at a time
  3. Many were concerned for their health and wanted to make sure they were safe

User Stories
Over the course of my conversations with them, I started to better understand the commonalities between each of our chats and compiled their collective thoughts into 2 user stories. The Lab Leads were essential here since they plan a majority of the meetings help staff, make key decisions, and connect Lab Staff with Internal WSIB.

Lab Staff ‚Äî ‚ÄúAs a Lab Staff Member, I want to be able to notify my Lab Lead when I will be coming into the Lab 48 hours in advance at least, and be able to select where I will be sitting during the day.‚Äù

Lab Lead ‚Äî ‚ÄúAs a Lab Lead, I want to be able to request attendance in-person for better team coordination, and to ensure Lab Visits are done safely. It's important for me to know why they want to attend in-person.‚Äù
Goal
Speaking with both parties helped me better understand the nuances in the need of Lab Staff and Lab Leads. I decided to shift the focus from pure contact tracing to more of a general app, focused on scheduling and safety.
Brief
An in-lab solution will provide the lab staff with a tool to know when to come into the office to limit in-person interactions between the Lab Leads, Design Team, Development Team, and Communications Team. It will be a mobile product used to transition back to in-person work while complying with Communitech‚Äôs guidelines whenever the Ontario Province decides to do so.

Requirements
  1. The need to see whos coming into the office
  2. The ability to see how long each staff member is staying for
  3. Wanting to book a time to come into the office
  4. See spots that are taken / who's attended
  5. Leverage contact tracing to ensure safety within the Lab and Communitech
What matters in our experience?
Viewing and Booking Attendance
Office Attendance and Seat Selection were key ideas that our users found important. I wanted to design an intuitive experience that takes into account many people booking as well as their reasoning. I quickly created a user flow and a low-fidelity mock-up to implement a Maze test to gain some staff insights.
Early Prototyping
During the initial hi-fi stages for early iterations, I also played around by prototyping advanced interactions and animations in InVision. Here we can see what selecting/viewing a seat could look like!
Improvements and Additions
A majority of the staff loved the idea of being able to view who is attending on what day and their reasoning. They also found the experience of booking convenient and Seat Selection to be intuitive. However, there some key issues and additions requested by the development team and the communications staff.

  1. Our Lab has gradually gained many more seats through the years. The seat selection view is not scalable to more seating options, and would become very complex quickly.

  2. We would need an onboarding process linking our work emails to implement profiles, and a way for the Lab to know the procedures within Communitech.

  3. A screener survey to gauge ones health and way for the user to be able to turn on and off contact tracing when in the Lab.
Design Iterations
For the next set of iterations in our ideation phase, I worked closely with another designer to rapidly mock-up, prototype and test different versions of the above features listed. We tried to be as creative as possible when designing these experience. I acted as the Master Designer in the end and merged all changes to helped us hone in on final design.

During this time, the development team also switched from developing strictly for mobile to making a PWA downloadable on your smartphone.
Style Guide and Supporting High-Fidelity
To ease the implementation of the final design into a PWA, I created a design system for the application. This would help future designers add to the experience while keeping consistency, and allows the developers to modularize the components and styles they need. The design system focused on simplicity and accessibility.
The Final Product
The Final product was a collection of many iterations, user tests, and cross-collaboration between different teams. A key emphasis was focused on onboarding and viewing/booking reservations to come into the Lab.

One of the most important parts was of the experience was the screener survey paired with contact tracing. This allowed people to make sure they were healthy before they came in, and allowed us to trace back the person with COVID initially in the case of an outbreak.
Retrospective
Successes
  1. The ability to implement quick user testing and interviewing practices within the Lab

  2. Assigning design roles for quicker collaboration enables for quick knowledge of who is coming into the office (Cool ideas for Integrations with Slack and Google Calendar)

  3. Interest from Communitech in taking this app to a greater scale (Communitech wide to be used by other)

  4. Ideation and Final Design implementation finished within 2 weeks before the new Lab Cohort came in

Improvements / Thoughts for next time
  1. A newer version of Safe@Lab (called Passport) retains some of the features Safe@Lab has (onboarding, surveys) and has been implemented into internal WSIB. However, Safe@Lab has not been fully utilized by the Lab Staff as Ontario continues to shift from Stage 3 to Stage 2 and vice-versa ‚Äî this means that returning to in-person work may not happen anytime soon

  2. Hence, Safe@Lab needs some more real-world usage within the Lab and more consistent data to see which aspects of the experience are being utilized the most

  3. Since this is the first completed version of the app, we'd need to make subsequent iterations to compare the improvements between and old and new design, as well as adoption rate amongst Communitech employees and other the Innovation Labs (like TD, Fairventures, and more)
