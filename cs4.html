Optimizing the process for reporting maintenance issues in schools and universities.
Role
Product Designer
Timeline
Winter 2020
Tools
Figma
Context
During the Winter 2020 recruiting season, I had the chance to complete the Google Design Challenge of their UX Design Internship selection. Out of two prompts, the prompt I chose revolved around wanting to create an experience that helps students report different maintenance and facility issues around their school or campus.

Looking back at the project now, there are a lot of design decisions I disliked, and ideas I wanted to incorporate, with a more thought out process. I wanted to completely redesign my experience (UpKeep) from Winter 2020 and remake it the term (Fall 2020).

Through user research, I was able to realize the many holes in the reporting process. For example, many students don’t have the know-how of who or where to report to. In addition, any reporting or communication had to be done via phone or email. This provided an excellent opportunity for me to delve into the problem.
Problem Statement
Two options were given as prompts, with the one I chose being the only one displayed here. The original prompts also contained more information which was not included here.

“Your school wants to improve the upkeep of campus facilities by creating a new system for reporting any facilities that may need maintenance or repair. Design an experience that allows students to report building or equipment issues on campus. Consider the process of those filing the report and of those receiving and taking action on the issues.”
The Current Reporting Process
I decided to start researching into how incident reporting works at my university. The image below is the only page on the university website describing the current reporting process for my school. I noted down some interesting assumptions about this process that would need validation.

  • Contact through only phone extension or email
  • Emails may be left unread or may take long to respond to
  • Maintenance may take a long time to be alerted of issues
  • User is not aware of resolved solutions / when they are resolved
Research
Before we start to solve anything, we want to be able to break down the key information to get to the its root causes. This included which campus facilities would be targeted, what the current reporting process and mediums are like. In addition, I did some further research onto the information provided by my university and other university maintenance departments.

We want to be able to make it easier to report maintenance or repair issues. However, we must ask about the current reporting process and whether students know about the reporting process. Through my research, I found that there is simply a disconnect between the student and the idea of reporting an issue in general.

In addition to conducting preliminary research, I spoke with many students and staff about the issue. In short, many students simply do not want to report on-campus issues. Staff noted that the current process to manage reports was quite cumbersome and tedious, but was manageable with how few reports are sent.
Student Pains and Wishes
After interviewing a variety of students (Computer Science, Math, Urban Planning), I was better able to understand their experiences and pain points. This would help us craft a better solution when we start to ideate.

Takeaways
  1. Don't know who or where to report to

  2. It's easier to simply take another door / exit or go to another washroom / water fountain than to think about reporting in general (Easier to avoid the issue and find an alternative than report and wait)

  3. When students we're asked about their expectations for repair, all students expected the repairs to be complete within days

  4. No students expect any sort of confirmation about the repair being attended to, but commented on wanting to know when it was finished
Staff Pains
I quickly realized that if someone was reporting the incident, that I should also speak with the other party which handles incoming reports. I spoke directly with the front desk workers in the student life center area which handling and handoff these types of indicents.

Takeaways
  1. Receiving student reports through email and phone is quite cumbersome

  2. Less urgent inquiries can be sent through email

  3. Important inquiries require a phone call which then require dispatching to janitorial / construction staff

  4. When students we're asked about their expectations for repair, all students expected the repairs to be complete within days

  5. Few reports are done by calling or email made directly. Most students report to the front desk physically or tell a nearby janitor. This is not the most scalable option in the case of many reports (like during a snowstorm)
Our Problem Space
  • Current reporting system is unknown to many, and unintuitive
  • Maintenance takes time depending on the issue and reports
  • User is not aware of resolved solutions / when they are resolved
Mission Statement
Ultimately, speaking with students and staff made realize this was a two-faced solution. However, since few reports were coming in to begin with, I decided on the following mission statement to help increase relevant incident reporting and shift focus to just students. We can achieve this with the following goals in mind below.

Get more students to report maintenance issues and failure.

  1. Provide a place to make, view, and understand the maintenance-issue reporting process (no more wonder who or where to report to)

  2. Make the reporting process quick, efficient, and easy

  3. Keep the user in the process by updating them on the status of their report
Exploration
In addition, I decide to map user flow of the experience and ideate through flows and wireframes. After 2 to three iterations, I came up with a basic skeleton of the features that would be present in an MVP.
Final designs
Key Decisions
Stretch Goal
After creating the UpKeep experience for enhancing the way students and staff alike report maintenance repair issues on campus, I believe it is important to look back at our original problem statement and solution and see how we could leverage our implement technologies into our problem.

Using Google's own resources, Google Maps already provides excellent location and landmarking within schools and universities. Since the initial experience utilizes some sort of mapping technology, we can always leverage Google Maps as a tool to showcase the maintenance issues and reports in-progress on the map.

In addition, we can leverage and even integrate technology like Google Crowdsource to better improve the landmark tracing when reporting issues. This would help UpKeep automatically find your location quicker so that your reporting becomes even more seamless.
Reflection
Redesigning my experience from a couple months ago helped me realize the importance of having empathy, validating ideas, and going above and beyond to really see how this product could integrate into the Google ecosystem.

It was a pleasure to remake my idea - I had a lot of fun doing so. But as with any project, there were lessons to be learned about the things I did well, and that of which I could improve on. I'm sure this product will help get more students to report maintenance issues and failure.

Successes
  1. I managed to address all of the problems mentioned at the beginning (Reporting Process, Categorizing Prompts, and Action Taken Confirmations)

  2. Incorporated lots of user research into design (report flow, reporting types and options) to validate assumptions

  3. Ended up making an entire new and visually stunning experience for reporting incidents at schools that’s easy to use and accessible (follows WCAG)

Learnings and Improvements
  1. 5 days spent on the research process allowed for excellent validation, but could've been expedited using online tools (Maze, UserInterviews, Google Forms)

  2. For a two-sided problem, I would in the future, also design an experience that would benefit the Staff who take in these reports (a dashboard design for example)

  3. Dispatching reports was also a problem mentioned by Staff, so another whole set of ideas could be used to design a portal or dashboard for receive these reports
